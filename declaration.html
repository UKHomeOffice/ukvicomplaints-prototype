Declaration of consent

To make a complaint on behalf of someone else you must have their explicit consent, or consent from the Home Office to handle this information.

Having a signed and dated agreement from the complainant is recommended, as evidence may be required at a later date.

If you make a complaint on behalf of someone else without their prior consent, or without approval from the Home Office, you will be committing an unlawful act, as described in section 55 of the Data Protection Act 1998.
